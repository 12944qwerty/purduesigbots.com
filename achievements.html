Purdue SIGBots Awards and Achievements

Purdue SIGBots has accomplished a lot in the team's history as result of an emphasis on innovation and industry-like experience. A few of the most noteworthy innovations and achievements are listed below, as they received recognition from the Vex U Robotics Competition judging staff.

2011/12 - Gateway

The team placed second overall in the third annual Vex Robotics All Star Challenge, earning the Tournament Finalist and Judges' Award trophies.

Photo courtesy of: https://www.cs.purdue.edu/news/11-16-11_SIGBOTs.html

2012/13 -Sack Attack

The 2012-13 season marked the release of PROS (Purdue Robotics Operating System), along with a complimentary "hunting" algorithm and a visualization tool for autonomous programming. As a result of this success, Purdue SIGBots earned the Innovation Award at the Vex College Challenge World Championships, indicating that the team had produced the most innovative design solution amongst the group of teams in the Vex College Championship league. In addition, these innovations helped lead to a 2nd place finish in the programming skills section of the competition.

2013/14 - Toss Up

The 2013-14 season was one with a heavy emphasis on electronics and software development. The Watchdog sensor, a custom sensor used to monitor motor current draw to prevent stalling, received a form factor upgrade and the R.A.D.I.U.S Array was created, allowing for significantly better line tracking capability than with the standard Vex sensors. These developments earned the team a second consecutive Innovate Award at the Vex U World Championships.
